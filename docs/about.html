About: Tracking Crime and Punishment

Important Terms:

  • Per Capita: A statistic relative to the overall population within a state our the country
  • Murder: The act of ending another's life in a premeditated, or thought out manner
  • Manslaughter: The act of ending another's life in either an unintentional or non-premeditated(instictive) manner
  • Aggravated Assault: The act of harming another individual with the intent/consequence of inflicting serious injury, or the act of attacking another individual using a weapon
  • Property Crime: Any crime in which another person's property is stolen or destroyed
  • Robbery: The unlawful seizure of property by means of force or threat of force
  • Burglary: The act of entering private property without legal permission with the intent of committing crimes such as theft
  • Grand Theft Auto: take someone else's vehicle, worth $950 or more, without permission, with the intent to deprive the true owner of the vehicle.
  • Rape: Any sexual act committed by one person on another without the victim's consent

Lo-Fi Wireframe Sketches:


About

My macro summary page includes a large map of the United States in which each state is shaded according to the prison count of that state. Below on the map includes a graph of the federal data such as various crimes over the years from 2000 to 2016. Furthermore, each state will have buttons that link to the micro summary for that specific state, and hovering over the state will provide the actual prisoner count for that state. My micro summary page includes a map of the selected state and includes various graphs on different types of data over the years.

Critique

My macro summary does not provide much data about the types of crime. Additionally, there are drop down menus that allow users to customize the presented data, and there are no signifiers that can provide users with how to use our software; instructions should be included for people to understand our tool. My micro summary provides a lot of information regarding the type of crime within a state but is not efficient in allowing users to choose their data. There should be an option that allows users to select the years, types of data, and also the state data in comparison to the federal data. Also, the map of the state is not very relevant.

Discoverability:

Affordances - The design allows users to click on different states which provides an overall micro summary of that state in relation to the federal data (macro summary)
Signifiers - The design provides clickable buttons with the state name to signify that the button can be clicked to provide more information (leads to micro summary)
Constraints - The design currently does not provide any constraints
Mappings - The design currently does not provide any mappings
Feedback - The design currently does not provide any feedback

Usability:

Accesibility - The design has not considered differently-abled individuals
Ease - The design can be used efficiently with the buttons and drop down menu in place
Tolerance - The design does not take into account user risk, however, the product should not cause any harm
Flexiblity - The design incorporates a drop down menu, allowing users to see select types of data
Perceptibility - The design does not yet include anything related to accessibility
Comfort - The design seems to be an appropriate size and space; this will be modified when we create the map and data
Iterability - Yes, the design can evolve without being completely done; the only difference between the next version is including more easy to use features

Combination of Design Elements

We have combined our ideas to come together on the following concept for our page

The macro-summary contains a map of the United States, with an additional dropdown to switch between metrics such as raw prison population, prisoners per capita and murders per capita
When the user clicks on a state, a MicroSummary will appear, displaying several graphs showing over time data from 2001 to 2016

LucidChart Graphic

Our pipeline is relatively simple. We have specific datasets for the macro and microsummaries to process and compile, and that processing happens alongside the creation of input dropdowns to allow the user to navigate the site.
